Sacraments

Initiation of Adults

Scroll

‍

The Catholic Church joyfully welcomes adults and older children who seek to be fully initiated into the faith, responding to God’s call to live as disciples of Christ. This journey of initiation is rooted in the early Church’s tradition of careful preparation and spiritual formation. Unlike Baptism, where the sacraments are celebrated first and their meaning unfolds over time, older candidates engage in a process of learning, prayer, and discernment before receiving the sacraments of initiation.

The Rite of Christian Initiation of Adults (RCIA) guides this journey, which unfolds in distinct stages over the course of a year or more. It begins when individuals express their desire to learn about the Catholic faith and be joined to the Church. As catechumens, they are accompanied by a sponsor who supports and encourages them in their spiritual growth.

Through ongoing formation, catechumens explore the teachings of Christ, the sacramental life, and the call to live as members of His body, the Church. When the parish community, sponsors, and catechists discern that a catechumen is ready, they enter a period of more intense preparation during Lent. This sacred time of prayer, fasting, and reflection leads to the celebration of the sacraments of Baptism, Confirmation, and the Eucharist, which take place at the Easter Vigil—the most solemn and joyous liturgy of the Church’s year.

The Easter Vigil, rich in symbolism and grace, is where catechumens are reborn in baptism, sealed with the Holy Spirit in confirmation, and united fully with Christ through the Eucharist. The period following their initiation, called mystagogy, is a time of deepening their understanding of the mysteries they have received and fully participating in the life of the parish.

For individuals already baptised in another Christian Church, the process is adapted to their circumstances. Their baptism is recognised as valid, and they are welcomed into full communion with the Catholic Church through confirmation and the Eucharist, honouring the unity of all Christians in Christ.

Many are drawn to the Catholic faith through experiences in Catholic schools, relationships with Catholic family members, or interactions with parish communities. At Sts Peter and Paul, it is a blessing each year to celebrate the initiation of new members during the Easter season, as they join in the mission and life of the Church.

If you feel called to explore the Catholic faith or to begin the journey of initiation, we warmly invite you to contact the parish office.
