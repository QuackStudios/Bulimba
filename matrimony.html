Matrimony

Scroll

‍

Marriage is a sacred covenant, where a man and woman vow to unite in a lifelong partnership of love and fidelity. In the Catholic Church, this solemn commitment takes place within the sacred space of a church, where the couple asks God to bless and consecrate their union. Marriage is not only a personal promise but also a sacrament, a visible sign of God’s grace and the love of Christ for His Church, as expressed in Ephesians 5:25: “Husbands, love your wives, just as Christ loved the Church and gave himself up for her.”

At the heart of the sacrament of marriage is the exchange of consent, where the bride and groom freely promise to love, honour, and cherish one another all the days of their lives. This consent, given before God and the Church, forms the foundation of the marriage covenant. The priest or deacon, acting as the Church’s witness, ensures the validity of this sacred bond, assists the couple in their preparation, and presides over the wedding liturgy, celebrating their union in the presence of the community of faith.

The Church views marriage as a vocation, a calling from God to reflect His love in the world through the intimate partnership of husband and wife. It is a relationship of mutual support, openness to life, and commitment to growing together in holiness. The sacrament provides the grace needed to live out this vocation, strengthening the couple to face life’s joys and challenges with faith and perseverance.

Parishioners who wish to celebrate their marriage in the church of Sts Peter and Paul are encouraged to contact the parish office at least six months before their desired wedding date. This allows time for adequate preparation, including participation in a marriage preparation program, which helps couples reflect on the responsibilities and spiritual significance of their commitment.

The church is also available to couples from outside the parish who wish to marry here. In such cases, it is necessary for the parish priest of the bride or groom, or another priest or deacon, to take responsibility for the preparation and celebration of the wedding.

For more information or to begin the process of preparing for the sacrament of marriage, please contact the parish office.
