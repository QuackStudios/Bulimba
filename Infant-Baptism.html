Infant Baptism

Scroll

‍

Celebrating baptism is a profound joy, not only for the child’s parents and siblings but for the entire parish community. Baptism is the first of the sacraments of initiation and is described by the Catechism of the Catholic Church as the "basis of the whole Christian life" (CCC 1213). Through baptism, a person is freed from original sin, becomes a member of God’s family, the Church, and is united with Christ in His death and resurrection (Romans 6:3-4). It marks the beginning of a lifelong journey of discipleship, answering the call to follow Christ.

In the parish of Sts Peter and Paul, we recognise the communal nature of this sacrament, scheduling the Celebration of Baptism twice each month. Before their baptism, the children are introduced to the parish community, who symbolically embrace them into the Church by signing them with the cross during a weekend Mass. This gesture reflects the words of Jesus in Mark 10:14: "Let the little children come to me, and do not hinder them, for the kingdom of God belongs to such as these." Parents and godparents, who play a vital role in nurturing the faith of the newly baptised, are invited to attend a baptism preparation session in the parish hall after Sunday Mass (from 9 am to 10 am). This session provides an opportunity to reflect on the significance of the sacrament, the liturgy of baptism, and the sacred responsibilities it entails. It is also a chance to meet other families who will be celebrating this milestone together.

Each year, the parish gathers for a special blessing during Sunday Mass to honour and pray for all those baptised in the parish over the previous twelve months. This communal act of prayer serves as a reminder that baptism is not a private event but a celebration of incorporation into the body of Christ, the Church, which St. Paul calls "the pillar and foundation of the truth" (1 Timothy 3:15). Christian parents, as the first educators of their children in the faith, are supported in this role by godparents. According to Church teaching, at least one godparent must be a confirmed Catholic who can serve as a witness to the faith and guide the child in living a Christian life. Other godparents must also be baptised Christians who understand and support this mission.

Parishioners wishing to arrange a baptism are warmly invited to contact the parish office.
